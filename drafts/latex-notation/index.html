Wrangling your LaTeX notation

29 Aug, 2021

PL papers tend to define lots of notation. Has the benefit of being able to express ideas succinctly and clearly. But there are downsides

If done in an ad hoc way it becomes hard to manage. For the writer,'it's hard to spot mistakes especially when rewriting papers. And use consistent symbols. And remembering what symbols have been used

For the reader, the problem is mostly remembering what notation the author has used. But mistakes are annoying and throw them off

Current state

So a common thing to do is to define all the notation upfront in a single place

This works well.

This got me thinking. Since latex is good at cross referencing Since weee doing this already. Why not ekevrrage that and make the links between uses and definitions concrete? This lets users navigate more easily, readers to remember things, and writers to jump. Most PDFs are electronic anyway. Why be content with a picture of a piece of paper

Introduce solution. Write a mall set of macros tk do this

Defining relations

Working with definitions. Not everything is introduced in a formal definition. Stuff implicitly like in a grammar saying x is a var without any other notation than just writing the nonterminal
