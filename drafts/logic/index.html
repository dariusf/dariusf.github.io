What is a logic?

11 Jan, 2022

it’s perhaps embarrassing that i couldn’t really give a good answer for this until a while ago. i believe being able to give intuition for something is the hallmark of understanding.

in the spirit, here’s an informal introduction to the big picture of logic, attempting to make sense of all the pieces, and prepare you for reading a more formal and thorough textbook. the aim is to be succinct and inexhaustive, as preparation for reading a book like huth and ryan or the handbook of automated reasoning.

this is a question i take for granted. it’s many things. wikipedia starts with it’s a formal system with syntax and semantics, blah. but that’s not really a satisfactory answer, because what we mean is what does it do and what is it used for. for our purposes in verification, a logic is a language for describing things of interest. specifying systems and writing properties. checking. decision procedures. but here are a few. prop, fol, ltl. expressing questions, decision problems.

rambling and informal introduction trying to connect my knowledge. it’s biased towards my areas of interest/understanding. static analysis, verification, my papers
